Our development strategy

Beam Support’s developmental strategy will not only incorporate a fully vetted team of professionals such as educators and mentors and 2 keyworkers who will provide 24 hours supervision within the residence but also provide a nurturing environment which will support a stimulating family environment.

The young people within our unit will be provided with opportunities for developing to their full potential. Their development will cover areas which include but are not limited to their emotional health, physical health, education and training, healthy social interactions and independent living skills, such as budgeting, shopping, managing bank accounts, cooking, cleaning and personal hygiene.

Social interactions will be encouraged through activities such as meeting with friends, playing board games with other young people within the unit, table tennis, going to the cinema, or attending local clubs and unit outings.

Our mentors

Our mentors are fully vetted and include educators, lawyers, and young people previously in care. All mentors will provide general guidance and advice that will support the young people in their integration within society facilitating independent living.

Mentors with a background in education will provide tuition in Maths, English and IT and advice on subjects that would support specific career paths.

Mentors with a legal background will familiarise the young people with the laws that govern behaviour in society.

We have a varied source of mentors who are backup carers within the foster care system who contribute to our mentorship programme which widens our range of vocational, formal and social skills advice and guidance.

Our Keyworkers

Our keyworker approach is to provide practical and emotional support that fosters trusting relationships between the keyworkers and the young people. Thereby enabling the young people with confidence which builds their self-esteem and helps them achieve the best outcomes.

The young people who are placed with Beam Support will have access to the different professionals according to their needs and interest. Each young person will receive a minimum of 10 hours per week, in a 1 on 1 support and guidance in the areas outlined above. This would enable them reach their full potential as individuals.

Our unit

Our unit is located in Harrow. Harrow is a leafy suburb of London and it is very cosmopolitan and can trace its roots back as far as King Henry VIII who spent considerable time here. This area of London offers beautiful scenery.

Harrow is 15 minutes away from Central London on the Metropolitan line from Harrow on the Hill tube station. There are a number of other options which include buses and trains.

Harrow town centre is less than a mile away and provides access to many restaurants, beauty shops, hair dressers, barbers, the cinema and shopping.

Our unit is located close to schools, Colleges of Further Education, Westminster University, Northwick Park Hospital, St Anne’s and St George’s shopping centres and a number of gyms. There is access nearby to parks and space to ride bikes and play football.

Within our semi-independent unit, each young person has their own bedroom with sufficient space to relax, play games or study. They have access to a shared kitchen, shared toilet and bathroom, a shared dining area and a lounge. There is also a second toilet on the ground floor and a staff only toilet on the second floor.

Our ethos is to promote the values of healthy family life style and provide young people with structure, personal space, respect and a relaxing environment which will foster a safe, comfortable and enabling family environment for young people.
